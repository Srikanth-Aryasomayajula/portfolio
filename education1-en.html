About the course

The course is an advanced curriculum that combines state-of-the-art computational methods with conventional mechanical engineering. It equips students for leadership positions in Industry 4.0 by providing specific expertise in fields including digital twins, artificial neural networks, and finite element methods. With two study tracks — Conception of Machines and Production of Machines—the curriculum integrates theoretical knowledge with real-world applications through research projects or internships. It covers digital design optimization, simulation tools, and lifecycle analysis.

About the university

Renowned for its inventiveness in engineering and applied sciences, RWTH Aachen University is one of Europe's top technological universities. The M.Sc. CAME program is overseen by the Institute of General Mechanics (IAM), which specializes in cutting-edge research in digital engineering, cyber-physical systems, and Industry 4.0. RWTH Aachen is well-known for its demanding curriculum and incorporation of state-of-the-art technology. Its alumni are in high demand due to their proficiency in simulation-driven engineering and computational tools.
